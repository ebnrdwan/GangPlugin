<details>
<summary>&nbsp;&nbsp;📋 <strong>Real output: PM Lead</strong></summary>

> **Bottom Line:** Ship 6 sections of the Stock Details Page in 5 weeks. Defer XGBoost Price Outlook and Market Regime Badge because the ML bugs are unresolved &mdash; broken predictions are worse than absent ones.
>
> **Key decisions:**
> 1. **MVP vs v2 split.** Six sections in v1, four deferred. The RICE scores expose the real finding: 5 of the 6 v1 sections have zero new backend work required.
> 2. **Critical path.** Build Indicators + Patterns + News + Header first (all unblocked today), then add the chart once the OHLCV endpoint is done. The page ships value before the chart lands.
> 3. **Free vs Premium gating.** AI recommendation badge is the Pro paywall anchor. Basic chart and indicators are free &mdash; gating standard indicators drives users to TradingView.
> 4. **ML deferred with re-entry condition.** XGBoost deferred until backtested accuracy exceeds 60% on a 90-day holdout.

</details>

<details>
<summary>&nbsp;&nbsp;🌐 <strong>Real output: Market Researcher</strong></summary>

> **Market Sizing:**
> - Bottom-up TAM: $1.35B (3M paying retail traders x $450/yr). The context brief's $8B figure conflates raw data licensing with the SaaS tools layer.
> - SAM: $840M&ndash;$1.26B. SOM Year 1: $600K&ndash;$3M ARR.
>
> **Competitive Scores (weighted):**
> TradingView: 4.35/5 &middot; TOS: 3.75/5 &middot; Trade Ideas: 3.35/5 &middot; TrendSpider: 3.15/5 &middot; StockInsight now: 2.55/5
>
> **Biggest Risk:** yfinance redistribution legal status &mdash; if exchange agreements prohibit redistribution to paying users, the fundamentals section requires a paid data provider, compressing margins.

</details>

<details>
<summary>&nbsp;&nbsp;🏗️ <strong>Real output: Solutions Architect</strong></summary>

> **Feasibility: 7/10.** Buildable in 6 weeks for a solo founder &mdash; with deliberate scope cuts.
>
> **What the code review revealed:**
> - `TradingChart.tsx` is fully built with dark theme and volume histogram. Just not wired up &mdash; a half-day fix.
> - `MarketDataProvider` has a proper ABC. `get_historical()` exists. The OHLCV endpoint is a serialization exercise.
> - Symbol stored in component state, not URL. 20-minute fix that unblocks bookmarking &mdash; should be the first commit.
>
> **The "STRONG BUY 94%" badge is a lie.** `_derive_signal()` produces a ratio, not a calibrated probability. Ship as "Signal Confidence" with the honest value.

</details>

<details>
<summary>&nbsp;&nbsp;💰 <strong>Real output: Finance Analyst</strong></summary>

> **Data Licensing &mdash; promoted to Risk Score 20 (was 8).** Serving scraped Yahoo Finance data to paying users almost certainly violates ToS. Fix: $29/mo Polygon.io Basic. Must resolve before first paid user.
>
> **Kill Switches (7 conditions):** Binary, time-bound financial triggers with prescribed actions.
>
> **Stress test:** At -50% revenue, business remains cash-flow positive (costs only $1,074/mo). Real stress failure is founder motivation at below-market compensation &mdash; key-person risk, not solvency.

</details>

<details>
<summary>&nbsp;&nbsp;📈 <strong>Real output: Business Strategist</strong></summary>

> **Core argument:** The Stock Details Page is a three-job machine: **acquire** (free, indexable), **tease** (visible but locked), **retain** (sticky Pro features). Every section has a conversion role.
>
> **Pricing:** Free: 1D chart + indicators. Starter ($29): patterns + earnings. Pro ($49): AI badge + signals + position banner.
>
> **Critical Risk:** ML accuracy is load-bearing under the Pro tier. Do not gate revenue on broken features. Shipping broken AI signals is a trust-destroying event no marketing recovers from.

</details>

<br/>

> 💡 **Notice how experts independently converged** on the same risks (yfinance licensing, ML accuracy) and the same recommendation (defer ML, ship rule-based first) &mdash; without seeing each other's work. This is role-isolated parallel analysis.

## Why Multi-Agent Debate

Single-agent AI gives you one perspective. That's a brainstorming partner, not a board.

Multi-agent debate is [proven to reduce hallucinations](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s44443-025-00353-3) and surface hidden assumptions.

<table>
<tr>
<td width="20%"><strong>🔒 Role Isolation</strong></td>
<td>Experts analyze independently before seeing each other's work &mdash; prevents anchoring bias</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><strong>⚔️ Adversarial Review</strong></td>
<td>Formal critique with pre-mortems and stress-tests, not "what do you think?"</td>
