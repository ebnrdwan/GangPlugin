The Problem

Building without structured analysis is expensive

Most product decisions are made by one person, one meeting, or one gut feeling — skipping the adversarial analysis that separates good ideas from great ones.

⚠️
Blind spots at launchNo structured devil's advocate — critical risks surface after shipping.
💸
Expensive reworkMarket fit assumptions go untested until after build cost is sunk.
🔇
Siloed expertisePM, Finance, UX, Architecture rarely debate the same brief simultaneously.
📉
No audit trailDecisions can't be revisited because reasoning was never documented.
The Solution

A committee of expert agents that argue, score, and decide

Gang assembles 6–9 specialist AI agents, runs them through a structured debate pipeline, and produces a rubric-anchored Go/No-Go recommendation with full evidence trail.

/gang advise
🏛️
6-Stage Pipeline — INIT → THINK → DEBATE → SCORE → ADVISE → DELIVER
🔍
Evidence-Backed — Every claim linked to a cited source, every score rubric-anchored
⚔️
Structured Debate — all-vs-all critique rounds with mandatory revision
💰
Cost-Aware — Budget limits, adaptive model routing, per-agent tracking
🎯
Configurable — Enable/disable agents, set weights, swap models

Evidence & Scoring

Every claim needs a citation

No hallucinated confidence. Gang enforces evidence linking at every layer — from position papers through final scoring.

Evidence Ledger
  • Auto-populated from codebase & web research
  • Confidence threshold filtering (default 0.5)
  • Claims must cite evidence_ids

  • JSON rubric anchors every numeric score
  • Assumptions flagged in justification
  • GO requires rubric-anchored scores

  • Downside scenarios always generated

Domain Expert Profiling

An expert shaped by your domain

During INIT, Gang interviews you with adaptive questions to build a hyper-specific domain expert — not a generic analyst, but a specialist calibrated to your industry niche.

TRADING EXAMPLE
P1a: Finance → P1b: Swing trading, Equities & Crypto
P2: Devil's Advocate → P3: Pattern-over-fundamentals
P4: Overfitting signals → P5: Sharpe >1.5, max-DD <15%
P1a
Domain Lens
What industry does this project live in? (Finance, Health, Legal…)
P1b
Sub-specialization (conditional)
For finance: trading style + asset class (equities/crypto/commodities)
P2
Evaluation Perspective
Devil's Advocate, Cautious Regulator, or Optimistic Operator?
P3
Known Biases
What common biases should the expert stress-test in your domain?
P4
Niche Pitfalls
Mistakes experts make in your specific niche that generalists miss?
P5
Success Benchmarks
Real success metrics in your domain (e.g., Sharpe >1.5, LTV/CAC >3)
UX Delta Pass
